 

Group Personal Accident and Work Injury Benefits Act

Work Injury Benefit Act steps in to lift the employer’s financial obligation under the Work Injury Benefit Act to compensate workers injured out of and in the course of employment. It includes:
       | - Cost of medical care
       | - Rehabilitation
       | - Lost wages for injured employees and
       | - Benefits for dependants of persons killed in work related accidents.


Note: “The provisions of the Contract of Insurance i.e. Work Injury Benefits Act Policy are based on the benefits payable and other terms and definitions provided for under the Work Injury Benefits Act, Chapter 13 of 2007 Laws of Kenya”.
Group Personal Accident (GPA) insurance covers employees against accidental loss of life or disability arising out of accident whilst on duty or pleasure on a 24 hours basis. 
The benefits provided for both WIBA and GPA are outlined as follows:-

Benefits
       | - Accidental Death - 8 Years Earnings
       | - Permanent Total Disablement (PTD) - 8 Years Earnings subject to continental scale
       | - Temporary Total Disablement (TTD) - Weekly Earnings up to a maximum of 52 weeks
       | - Medical expenses Kshs 500,000/- per person
       | - Funeral expenses Kshs 100,000/= per deceased employee
       | - Occupational diseases as per WIBA Act up to a maximum of Kshs 5,000,000 per employee
       | - Accumulation limit = Kshs. 150,000,000 per person, and Kshs. 300,000,000 in aggregate
